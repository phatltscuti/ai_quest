# Southeast Asia EdTech Market Report 2025

**Published by:** SEA Digital Education Research Institute (SEDRI)
**Date:** December 2025
**Scope:** Vietnam, Thailand, Indonesia, Philippines, Malaysia, Singapore

---

## Market Overview

The Southeast Asian EdTech market reached **$12.8 billion** in 2025, growing at a CAGR of 18.3% since 2022. The market is projected to reach **$23.5 billion by 2028**, driven by government digitalization programs, smartphone penetration, and increasing demand for AI-powered learning tools.

## Key Trends

### Trend 1: AI-Powered Personalization

AI-driven adaptive learning is the fastest-growing segment, reaching **$3.2 billion** in 2025 (25% of total market). Key developments:
- 67% of EdTech companies in SEA now offer some form of AI personalization
- Average learning outcome improvement: 15-30% for AI-personalized vs. traditional digital content
- Major players: EduNova (Vietnam), Ruangguru (Indonesia), Topica (Vietnam), Quipper (Japan/SEA)

### Trend 2: Mobile-First Learning

- **89% of EdTech users** in SEA access content primarily via mobile devices
- Offline-capable apps growing rapidly in rural areas (Indonesia, Philippines, Vietnam)
- Short-form content (5-15 min lessons) preferred by 73% of users
- Mobile learning app downloads in SEA: 450M in 2025 (up 34% from 2024)

### Trend 3: Micro-credentials and Skills-Based Learning

- Corporate EdTech spending grew **42% YoY** to $3.1B
- 58% of employers in SEA now accept micro-credentials for hiring decisions
- Top skills in demand: AI/ML, data analytics, digital marketing, project management
- Average corporate learning budget: $350/employee/year (up from $240 in 2023)

### Trend 4: Government Digital Education Programs

| Country | Program | Investment | Impact |
|---------|---------|-----------|--------|
| Vietnam | "Digital School 2025-2030" | $500M | 10,000+ schools to digitize by 2027 |
| Indonesia | "Merdeka Belajar Digital" | $380M | Free AI-powered learning for 52M students |

## Challenges

1. **Digital Divide:** 30-40% of students in rural SEA lack reliable internet; offline solutions critical
2. **Teacher Readiness:** Only 35% of teachers in SEA feel confident using AI tools in classroom
3. **Content Quality:** AI-generated educational content requires rigorous quality assurance
4. **Data Privacy:** Diverse regulations across ASEAN countries create compliance complexity
5. **Monetization:** Willingness to pay remains low in price-sensitive markets (ARPU $3-15/month range)
